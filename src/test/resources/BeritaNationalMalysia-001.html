Melaka Biotech, Prathista To Undertake RM40 Million Joint-Venture Research Project

MELAKA, March 12 (Bernama) -- Melaka Biotech Corp will team up with India's Prathista Industries Ltd to undertake a RM40 million joint-venture research project to produce organic fertilisers.

Chief Minister, Datuk Seri Idris Haron, said the two-phase joint-venture would last 18 months.

He said phase one would involve an investment of RM18 million to produce organic fertilisers for agricultural, aquaculture and fishing industries and second phase, worth RM22 million, aimed to produce liquid and granular organic fertilisers.

"Prathista will use the fermentation research facility to use innovative technology from India and US.

"This project is based on zero-pollution concept and will be environmentally-friendly as the chemicals use in the process will cause no harm," he told reporters after the signing of the memorandum of understanding (MOU) between Melaka Biotech and Prathista Industries Ltd here today.

Idris said 50,000 tonnes of fertilisers would be produced a year and 200 jobs would be created in the state.

At the same event, Melaka Biotech also signed an MOU with Universiti Teknologi Malaysia to develop natural resources-based products, specifically nutraceutical, health and cosmetics.

He said the joint venture also involved extraction of raw materials, product formulation and research contract with the university.

"Melaka Biotech will also sponsor its staff to undertake post-graduate studies in bioprocess engineering at the university," he said.
