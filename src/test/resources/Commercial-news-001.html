Maybank bullish on Oldtown’s expansion plans



0 comments

MAYBANK Research is bullish on Oldtown Bhd stock, given the company’s plans to spread its wings further into China.

 According to Maybank, China has overtaken Hong Kong to be Oldtown’s biggest and fastest growing export market for its fast moving consumer goods (FMCG) products.

 “We maintain our earnings forecasts and ‘buy’ rating, with an unchanged target price of RM2.67, based on 21 times current year 2015 price earnings ratio,” Maybank said in its research note.

 Maybank is confident that the FMCG division will continue to see rapid growth, given its strong market position in  key markets.

Maybank  is  positive that Oldtown is setting up its flagship online store under the biggest B2C e-commerce portal in China, TMall.com, to extend its presence on the mainland.

 “In 2013, the white coffee market in Malaysia and Hong Kong grew 27 per cent and 19 per cent respectively. Oldtown commands the biggest market share in both countries. In Malaysia, Oldtown’s café business is moving towards the quick service restaurant concept,” said the research house.

 Maybank  said as for the food and beverage business, Oldtown now has four types of layouts, namely the grand café, signature café, generic café and kiosk.
