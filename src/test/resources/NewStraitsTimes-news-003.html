'I'm a stronger underdog'

By SHAHRUM SAYUTHI | news@nst.com.my

0 comments

CAMPAIGNING: Chew Mei Fun feels more confident based on good reception

KAJANG: AS official campaigning for the Kajang by-election reached the halfway point today, Barisan Nasional  candidate Datin Paduka Chew Mei Fun admitted that she felt more confident about springing an upset against her opponent, PKR president Datuk Seri Dr Wan Azizah Ismail.

Speaking after visiting a home for disabled women run by Ikhsan Welfare Association here yesterday, Chew said she remains the "underdog candidate" compared with Dr Wan Azizah as the Kajang state constituency has been PKR's stronghold since the party won there in the general election in 2008.

"I am still wearing the underdog tag just like when I began the campaign. The only thing is now I feel that I am a stronger underdog," she said, adding that her confidence was based on the good reception she received from constituents during her campaign rounds. Chew said despite the odds stacked against her, she had entered the by-election as a candidate aiming to win and will do her best to convince Kajang voters to vote for her.

The state seat was vacated after then PKR assemblyman, Lee Chin Cheh resigned on Jan 27 in a move designed by the party to allow its leader Datuk Seri Anwar Ibrahim to contest the by-election and take over from Tan Sri Abdul Khalid Ibrahim as Selangor menteri besar. Anwar, however, was not named as PKR's candidate following the Court of Appeals decision to jail him for five years after finding him guilty of committing sodomy on March 7.

Asked about a recent claim by DAP supremo Lim Kit Siang that she will suffer a loss and lose her candidacy deposit, Chew said the 72-year old may say whatever he wanted as "this is a free country".
