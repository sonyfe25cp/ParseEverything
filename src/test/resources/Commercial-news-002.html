Sime Darby acquires 70pc in TFP Engineering



0 comments

KUALA LUMPUR: Sime Darby Bhd has acquired a 70 per cent stake in TFP Engineering Pty Ltd for A$3.2 million (about RM9.5 million).

Sime Darby said its indirect subsidiary, Haynes Mechanical Pty Ltd bought the stake from owners Gaylene Elaine Poke and Tony Francis Poke.

The sales and purchase agreement between the buyer and sellers was signed last month and will be funded by Haynes' internally generated funds and existing working capital overdraft facility.

"The acquisition will enable Haynes to expand its labour hire opportunities from its existing geographical presence in Queensland and Western Australia to the New South Wales coal basin, Tasmania and South Australia," Sime Darby said in a filing to Bursa Malaysia today.

The diversified conglomerate said the acquisition would also increase servicing opportunities through the utilisation of the "Pakka Jacks" system.

The "Pakka Jacks" system is a jacking lifting system that was designed and built by TFP Engineering, which incorporates an automated self-packing system that applies positive pressure during the lifting of large equipment such as excavators.-- Bernama
